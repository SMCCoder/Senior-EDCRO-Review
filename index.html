My Beginning

I came to A3 because my parents suggested it. They said it was a good place to go to because, it was small, with a welcome community.

As a deaf person who needed accomodations, it seemed ideal.

At that point, however, I was also anxious in social situations and I had no idea what I wanted to do for my carreer. Little did I know, A3 would change that.

My First Headwaters

I was a shy person when I first came to A3,

It was stressful at first, because I was surrounded by people I didn't know,

But then I came to know them. They were supportive, and wanted to make sure that they could hear me. At the end of it, there was a schoolwide conversation where everyone was going up and saying what they liked about Headwaters. Nervously, I went up, and thanked everyone for being so willing to accomodate me. It was that experience that helped solidify my confidence.

Learning Coding

One of my most impactful classes I had was Creative Coding. It made me interested in programming and problem-solving, and gave me a direction for my carreer.

Before, I didn't have any pacticular interests. However, I found that coding was something I wanted to even outside of class.

This help set the stage for what was to come.

The Internship

Because my mother knew I was interested in coding, she suggested I apply for an internship at the U of O. I applied, and got in.

In the summer of my Junior year, I applied for an internship at the University of Oregon, and got in.
